INFORMATION SECURITY POLICY — ACME Corp (v2.1, effective 2025-01-01)
======================================================================
Internal document — not for external distribution.

1. PURPOSE AND SCOPE
This policy establishes the information security requirements for all systems,
data, and personnel at ACME Corp. It applies to all employees, contractors,
and third-party service providers who access ACME systems or handle ACME data.
The Chief Information Security Officer (CISO) is responsible for maintaining
this policy and conducting an annual review.

2. ACCESS CONTROL
2.1  All users are assigned a unique username and password. Shared accounts are
     prohibited. Passwords must be at least 12 characters and include uppercase,
     lowercase, a digit, and a special character.
2.2  Multi-factor authentication (MFA) is mandatory for all remote access, all
     administrator accounts, and any system that stores personal data.
2.3  Access rights are granted on a least-privilege basis and reviewed every
     six months by the system owner. Permissions are revoked within 24 hours of
     an employee's departure.
2.4  Privileged access (root, admin) requires a separate account and must be
     documented in the access register.

3. DATA CLASSIFICATION AND ASSET MANAGEMENT
3.1  All information assets must be registered in the Asset Inventory maintained
     by IT Operations. Each asset is assigned an owner who is responsible for
     its classification and protection.
3.2  Data is classified as Public, Internal, Confidential, or Restricted.
     Confidential and Restricted data must not be stored on personal devices
     without explicit approval and encryption.
3.3  Assets leaving the organisation must be approved by the asset owner and
     logged. Media containing Confidential or Restricted data must be securely
     wiped or destroyed before disposal.

4. CRYPTOGRAPHY AND DATA PROTECTION
4.1  All data classified as Confidential or Restricted must be encrypted at
     rest using AES-256 or equivalent. Encryption keys are managed through the
     company key management system and rotated annually.
4.2  All data in transit must use TLS 1.2 or higher. Unencrypted protocols
     (FTP, HTTP, Telnet) are prohibited on production systems.
4.3  Cryptographic controls are reviewed annually to ensure they remain
     adequate given current threat intelligence.

5. INCIDENT MANAGEMENT
5.1  Any suspected or confirmed security incident must be reported to the
     Security Operations Centre (SOC) immediately using the incident reporting
     form at security@acme.com or the internal ticketing system.
5.2  All incidents are logged, triaged, and assigned a severity level (P1–P4)
     within two hours of detection.
5.3  A post-incident review is conducted for all P1 and P2 incidents within
     five business days. Findings and corrective actions are documented.
5.4  Personal data breaches affecting EU residents must be reported to the
     relevant supervisory authority within 72 hours of discovery.

6. VULNERABILITY AND PATCH MANAGEMENT
6.1  IT systems are scanned for vulnerabilities at least monthly using an
     approved scanning tool. Critical and high vulnerabilities must be
     remediated within 30 days. Medium vulnerabilities within 90 days.
6.2  Security patches are applied according to the Patch Management Procedure.
     Emergency patches for actively exploited vulnerabilities are applied
     within 48 hours.
6.3  Penetration tests are conducted at least annually for internet-facing
     systems and after significant changes.

7. NETWORK SECURITY
7.1  The network is segmented into zones (external DMZ, internal, management).
     Traffic between zones is controlled by firewalls with default-deny rules.
7.2  Remote access is permitted only through the company VPN with MFA enabled.
7.3  Network traffic is monitored and logs are retained for a minimum of 90 days.
7.4  Wireless networks use WPA3 encryption. Guest networks are isolated from
     the internal network.

8. OPERATIONS SECURITY
8.1  All production systems must have documented operational procedures covering
     startup, shutdown, backup, and error handling.
8.2  Antivirus and endpoint detection and response (EDR) software must be
     installed and kept up to date on all endpoints.
8.3  System logs (authentication, access, errors) are collected centrally and
     monitored. Logs are retained for at least 12 months and backed up offsite.
8.4  Production, test, and development environments are separated. Production
     data may not be used in development or test environments without
     anonymisation.
8.5  Capacity planning reviews are conducted quarterly to ensure systems can
     meet current and projected demands.

9. HUMAN RESOURCES SECURITY
9.1  Background checks are performed for all new employees in roles with access
     to Confidential or Restricted data prior to commencement.
9.2  All employees receive information security awareness training within 30 days
     of joining and annually thereafter.
9.3  Employees are required to read and sign this policy and the Acceptable Use
     Policy on joining. Violations may result in disciplinary action.
9.4  Upon termination or role change, access rights are reviewed and adjusted
     immediately. Company equipment must be returned on the last day.

10. BACKUP AND RECOVERY
10.1 All critical data is backed up daily. Backups are stored in an encrypted,
     geographically separate location.
10.2 Backup integrity is verified monthly through test restores. Results are
     documented in the Backup Register.
10.3 Recovery Time Objectives (RTO) and Recovery Point Objectives (RPO) are
     defined per system and reviewed annually.

11. THIRD-PARTY AND SUPPLIER SECURITY
11.1 All suppliers processing ACME data or having access to ACME systems must
     complete a security questionnaire before onboarding.
11.2 Contracts with suppliers must include data protection clauses aligned with
     applicable law and security requirements.
11.3 Supplier security posture is reviewed annually. High-risk suppliers may
     be subject to on-site audits.

12. PHYSICAL AND ENVIRONMENTAL SECURITY
12.1 Access to server rooms and data centres is restricted to authorised
     personnel. Entry is logged using badge readers.
12.2 Visitors to secure areas must be accompanied by an authorised employee
     and logged in the visitor register.

13. COMPLIANCE AND AUDIT
13.1 ACME will maintain compliance with applicable laws and regulations
     including GDPR, and relevant industry standards.
13.2 Internal audits of information security controls are conducted annually.
     Findings are reported to senior management and tracked to closure.
13.3 Employees must not deliberately attempt to circumvent security controls.
     Any identified weaknesses must be reported to the CISO immediately.
